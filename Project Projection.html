Strategy & Overview

Goals · Needs · Trends

Goals & Objectives

  • Launch a compliant, stable Android MVP focused on quote requests and responses.
  • Onboard a critical mass of local businesses with a free 90-day trial.
  • Deliver a frictionless experience where customers receive targeted quotes in minutes.
  • Lay the foundation for monetization via recurring B2B subscriptions and quote-based fees.

Key Needs

  • Fast, intuitive flows for customers to submit requests with photos and location radius.
  • Simple business tools to respond, showcase their store, and manage visibility.
  • Robust admin control for approvals, pricing configuration and compliance.
  • Clear privacy, permission, and billing communication to satisfy Google Play requirements.

Market Trends

  • Rising demand for real-time local price comparison without physically visiting stores.
  • SMBs looking for affordable, measurable digital channels beyond generic marketplaces.
  • Users expecting chat-like, mobile-native experiences with clear consent and transparency.
  • Growth of reverse auction and on-demand quote models in home services, mobility, and retail.

Target Audience

Personas · Segmentation
Primary Customer Persona

Urban Value Seekers

Mobile-native shoppers (ages 22–40) who want to compare prices for electronics, household goods, and services before committing to a purchase.

  • Comfortable uploading photos and using maps.
  • Expect fast responses and transparent pricing.
  • Highly influenced by convenience and savings.
Business Persona

Local Specialty Stores

Independent electronics, home goods, and service providers with limited marketing resources, looking for cost-effective customer acquisition.

  • Prefer simple dashboards and mobile notifications.
  • Open to subscription-based models if ROI is proven.
  • Value local targeting over broad, generic marketplaces.
Secondary Segments

Hyperlocal & Niche Sellers

Boutique stores, repair shops, and service professionals looking to respond quickly to high-intent leads within a defined radius.

  • Rely on repeat business and local reputation.
  • Benefit from structured quote history and analytics.
  • App helps them stay competitive on price and responsiveness.

Technical Details

Stack · Integrations · Architecture

Technology Stack

  • Android app: Kotlin/Java + Android SDK (Material 3 design system).
  • Backend: PHP (Laravel) or Node.js (REST APIs) with JWT-based auth.
  • Database: MySQL/PostgreSQL for structured user, business, and quote data.
  • Storage: Cloud object storage for product and store images.
  • Notifications: Firebase Cloud Messaging for real-time quote alerts.

Key Integrations

  • Google Play Services for geolocation and map-based store discovery.
  • Payment gateway (e.g., Stripe / local PSP) for B2B subscriptions.
  • Analytics for tracking quote funnel, conversions, and retention.

Feature Priority

Must-have · Should-have · Nice-to-have
Must-have (MVP)
  • Customer registration & login with consent and privacy policy.
  • Business registration, store profile, and basic verification.
  • Product/service request with text, images, and radius selection.
  • Quote responses from multiple businesses, visible in-app.
  • Admin approvals, category management, and pricing configuration.
  • Fully compliant permission handling and Play Store-ready build.
Should-have (Post-launch)
  • In-app ratings and basic feedback on quote quality.
  • Saved searches, favorite stores, and recurring requests.
  • Basic analytics dashboard for businesses (views, quote win rate).
  • Multi-language support for priority markets.
Nice-to-have (Later phases)
  • In-app messaging between customers and stores.
  • Advanced per-quote billing and tiered promotion slots.
  • AI-assisted suggested prices and smart matching.
  • Deeper reporting and exportable analytics for premium plans.

SWOT Summary

Strategic Snapshot
Strengths
  • Focused on fast local quotes instead of generic listings.
  • Clear MVP scope aligned with Google Play compliance.
  • Subscription-led revenue with low setup friction.
  • Flexible enough to support multiple verticals and regions.
Weaknesses
  • Requires critical mass of active businesses to feel valuable.
  • Early-stage reliance on a single platform (Android).
  • Quote quality and response times depend on partner discipline.
Opportunities
  • Expand to iOS and web once Android traction is validated.
  • Launch category-specific campaigns (electronics, services, groceries).
  • Introduce premium analytics and quote-based micro-fees.
Threats
  • Large marketplaces launching local quote features.
  • Regulatory changes in payment and data privacy standards.
  • Adoption lag in markets with low digital penetration.
